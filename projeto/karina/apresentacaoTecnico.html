The Shift: Control vs. Enablement

Historically, GPS tracking was viewed as a "Big Brother" mechanism. The modern approach flips this narrative. By using **Location Intelligence (LI)** and **Field Force Automation (FFA)**, we automate bureaucratic tasks (like check-ins) and optimize routes. This isn't about watching the seller; it's about giving them more time to sell.

Key Insight

A typical unoptimized seller spends up to 40% of their day in unproductive travel. Geospatial tools aim to reclaim this lost revenue time.

Advanced Visualization

A critical finding in the technical analysis is the depreciation of the native Google Maps Heatmap layer. To handle large historical datasets (e.g., a year of visits), we must adopt deck.gl.

Deck.gl utilizes WebGL to leverage the GPU, allowing the visualization of hundreds of thousands of data points without crashing the browser, whereas traditional CPU rendering struggles after a few thousand points.

Structure of Costs (2025-2026)

The Google Maps Platform operates on a "pay-as-you-go" model. Understanding the cost drivers is essential for ROI. The estimated monthly cost per seller ranges between $3.00 and $5.00.

Cost Optimization Strategies

  • 💾

    Geocoding Cache

    Store lat/long in your database. Never geocode the same client address twice.

  • ⏱️

    Session Tokens

    Group autocomplete keystrokes into a single session to reduce API calls significantly.

  • 📉

    Daily Quotas

    Set hard limits in the Google Cloud Console to prevent budget overruns from loops.

LGPD & Legal Compliance

Tracking employees in Brazil is legal but requires strict adherence to privacy laws (LGPD) and labor laws (CLT).

Transparency Principle Mandatory

Employees must be explicitly notified (Contract Addendum).

Purpose Limitation Mandatory

Tracking is only allowed during working hours. System must have "Privacy by Design" toggles.

Data Security Critical

Location history is personal data. Leaks are severe infractions.
